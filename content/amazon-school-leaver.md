# Amazon Apprenticeship — ApprenticeEdge Prep Pack

## The Programme

**What it is:** Amazon offers multiple apprenticeship programmes across the UK, from Level 3 to Level 6 (degree apprenticeship). The corporate programmes in Tech, Operations, and Finance are the most competitive.

**Length:** 15 months (Level 4) to 4 years (Level 6 degree)

**Salary:** £24,000–£32,000 starting. Amazon also offers stock (RSUs) to some apprentice cohorts.

**Locations:** London (corporate), Manchester, Edinburgh, plus fulfilment centres nationally

**Programmes available:**
- Software Development Engineer Apprenticeship (Level 6)
- IT Operations Technician (Level 3/4)
- Finance Apprenticeship
- HR Apprenticeship
- Marketing/Communications
- Operations (Fulfilment/Logistics management)

**Application window:** Opens throughout the year — Amazon recruits on a rolling basis, not a fixed academic calendar. Check Amazon Jobs UK directly.

## The Application Stages

### Stage 1 — Online Application
CV and application form. Amazon uses an ATS (Applicant Tracking System) heavily — your CV must contain keywords from the job description.

**Amazon-specific requirement:** Some applications ask you to answer questions specifically framed around Amazon's Leadership Principles (LPs). You must know all 16 LPs before you apply.

### Stage 2 — Online Assessments
- **Work Style Assessment** — personality/values alignment. Answer consistently with how you'd actually behave, not how you think Amazon wants you to behave.
- **Technical Assessment** (for tech roles) — coding problems in the language of your choice. LeetCode easy-to-medium level.

### Stage 3 — Phone/Video Screen
Usually with a recruiter or hiring manager. 30–45 minutes.

**Typical questions:**
- Walk me through a project or achievement you're proud of.
- Tell me about a time you had to deal with a difficult person. (Leadership Principle: Earn Trust)
- Describe a time you made a mistake. What did you learn? (LP: Learn and Be Curious)
- Tell me about a time you did more than was required. (LP: Bias for Action)

**Every question at Amazon maps to a Leadership Principle.** This is not an exaggeration. Know which LP each of your STAR stories demonstrates.

### Stage 4 — "Loop" (Amazon's Assessment Centre)
Amazon calls its final stage the "Loop" — a series of 1:1 interviews, typically 3–5 back-to-back, each with a different interviewer. One interviewer is designated the "Bar Raiser" — an independent Amazon employee whose sole job is to maintain hiring standards. The Bar Raiser has veto power.

**What to expect:**
- Each interviewer focuses on 2–3 Leadership Principles
- Expect the same LP to come up in multiple interviews — have more than one STAR story per principle
- Bar Raiser asks harder, more abstract questions — "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager and were right" is typical
- Interviewers share notes in a debrief and vote independently

## Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles — Know All of These

| # | Principle | What it means in practice |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Customer Obsession | Start with what the customer needs, work backwards |
| 2 | Ownership | Act like it's your company, not just your job |
| 3 | Invent and Simplify | Find new, simpler ways to do things |
| 4 | Are Right, A Lot | Make good judgements with incomplete information |
| 5 | Learn and Be Curious | Never stop learning; stay intellectually open |
| 6 | Hire and Develop the Best | Raise the bar; help others grow |
| 7 | Insist on the Highest Standards | Don't accept good enough |
| 8 | Think Big | Set bold goals, not just incremental ones |
| 9 | Bias for Action | Move fast; calculated risk-taking is valued |
| 10 | Frugality | Do more with less |
| 11 | Earn Trust | Be honest, transparent, self-critical |
| 12 | Dive Deep | Get into the details; don't just manage from above |

**Prepare at least one STAR story for each of:** Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action, Earn Trust, Deliver Results, Learn and Be Curious, Have Backbone. These come up most often.

## Amazon STAR Stories — What Makes Them Strong

Amazon wants **specific, measurable results.** Vague outcomes ("the team was happy", "things improved") will not score well.

**Strong result examples:**
- "Reduced the time to complete the task by 30%"
- "The event had 40% higher attendance than the previous year"
- "I identified an error that saved the team from resubmitting work"
- "My suggestion was adopted by the group and we delivered on time"

**Weak result examples:**
- "It worked out well in the end"
- "The team was pleased with the outcome"
- "I learned a lot from the experience"

## Commercial Awareness — What You Must Know About Amazon

### History in Brief

Jeff Bezos left his job at a New York hedge fund in 1994 and drove cross-country to Bellevue, Washington, writing the business plan in the passenger seat. He launched Amazon in July 1994 from his garage as an online bookstore — chosen because books were a product with universal demand, infinite SKUs, and no dominant online retailer. The company went public in 1997 at $18 per share. Over the following decade, Amazon expanded relentlessly into music, electronics, clothing, and ultimately everything.

The pivotal moment came in 2006 with the launch of Amazon Web Services (AWS), which turned Amazon's internal infrastructure into a product. AWS is now the world's largest cloud platform and accounts for the majority of Amazon's operating profit. Bezos stepped down as CEO in July 2021, handing the role to Andy Jassy — the executive who built AWS from scratch. Amazon's scale today is extraordinary: it is simultaneously a retailer, logistics company, cloud provider, media studio, and advertising platform.

**Revenue (FY2024):** ~$590bn. Amazon is one of the largest companies in history.

**What Amazon does:**
- **E-commerce:** The core retail business. Marketplace (third-party sellers) is now larger than first-party retail.
- **AWS (Amazon Web Services):** Cloud computing. ~$100bn revenue. The most profitable part of Amazon by far. Powers a huge portion of the internet.
- **Prime:** Subscription loyalty programme bundling delivery, video, music. ~200m global subscribers.
- **Advertising:** $50bn+ revenue. Amazon Ads is the third largest digital ad platform after Google and Meta.
- **Fulfilment/Logistics:** Amazon has built one of the most sophisticated logistics networks in the world — same-day delivery in major cities.
- **Alexa/Devices:** Smart home, Kindle, Fire TV.

**Current Amazon talking points (2025–26):**
- AWS and the GenAI infrastructure race vs. Google Cloud and Azure
- Amazon's last-mile delivery expansion (Amazon Logistics vs. Royal Mail/DPD)
- Regulatory pressure on Amazon's marketplace dominance (EU Digital Markets Act)
- Amazon's return-to-office mandate and workforce culture
- Project Kuiper (Amazon's satellite internet, competing with Starlink)
