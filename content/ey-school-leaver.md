# EY School Leaver Programme — ApprenticeEdge Prep Pack

## The Programme

**What it is:** EY School Leaver Programme — a higher apprenticeship combining professional qualifications with client work. EY is particularly strong on early responsibility and client contact.

**Length:** 3–4 years to chartered qualification

**Salary:** £24,000–£28,000 starting (London higher). EY publishes salary bands openly.

**Locations:** London (multiple offices), Birmingham, Bristol, Cambridge, Edinburgh, Leeds, Manchester, Reading

**Streams available:**
- Assurance (Audit)
- Tax
- Strategy and Transactions
- Consulting
- Technology
- Law (EY Law — relatively unique among Big Four)

**Qualification:** ACA (Assurance/Tax), or relevant professional qualification for other streams. All funded.

**Application window:** Opens September/October. EY recruits on a rolling basis — early application increases your chances significantly.

## The Application Stages

### Stage 1 — Online Application
Standard form with motivational questions. EY asks you to select your service line preference — research it properly before committing.

**EY values:** integrity, respect, teaming, inclusiveness, energy, enthusiasm. Know them. The application will reference them.

### Stage 2 — EY Online Assessments
EY uses a bespoke assessment platform:
- **EY Strengths Assessment** — not a pass/fail test, but used to match you to teams. Answer honestly — trying to game it makes your profile incoherent.
- **Numerical Reasoning** — data tables, graphs, percentages. Standard format.
- **Situational Judgement Test** — professional scenarios.

**Tip:** EY places heavy emphasis on strengths. Before the assessment, identify your genuine top 3 strengths and be ready to demonstrate them with examples.

### Stage 3 — Video Interview (EY uses HireVue)
5–6 questions, 30 seconds prep, 2-minute response window.

**Typical questions:**
- What does exceptional client service look like to you?
- Tell me about a time you worked with someone very different from yourself.
- Describe a situation where you had to learn something quickly.
- Why EY specifically — what is it about our approach that resonates with you?
- Tell me about a time you showed resilience when things didn't go to plan.

### Stage 4 — Assessment Centre (EY calls it the "Final Stage Assessment")
Usually a half day. Structure varies by service line but typically includes:

1. **Group exercise** — collaborative case study with 5–8 candidates. Assessors watch for listening, contribution quality, and how you handle disagreement.
2. **Individual case study presentation** — given material 20–30 mins beforehand. Present recommendation to an assessor, then take questions.
3. **Partner interview** — 45 min. Expect "tell me about yourself" as a narrative opener, followed by probing competency questions. EY partners ask follow-up questions — prepare for depth, not just breadth.

**EY Assessment Centre tips:**
- In the group exercise, quality of contribution beats quantity. Two sharp points beat five weak ones.
- In the partner interview, show genuine curiosity about EY's business — ask one smart question at the end.
- EY values "teaming" heavily — show you can amplify others, not just yourself.

## Commercial Awareness — What You Must Know About EY

**Revenue (FY2024):** $51bn globally. Typically second or third largest of the Big Four.

**What EY does:**
- **Assurance:** Audit of financial statements. EY audits major banks and insurers — strong financial services practice.
- **Tax:** Corporate tax, indirect taxes, international tax, private client.
- **Strategy and Transactions (SaT):** M&A advisory, valuations, integration. Formerly Transaction Advisory Services.
- **Consulting:** Business transformation, supply chain, people advisory.
- **Technology:** EY Tech Hub, digital transformation, cybersecurity.
- **EY Law:** Legal services — relatively unique in Big Four.

**Current EY talking points (2025–26):**
- EY's failed "Project Everest" — attempted split of audit and consulting (blocked by partners in 2023). Shows tension in Big Four governance — know this.
- EY's heavy investment in AI tools for audit (EY Atlas)
- Growth in financial services audit work
- ESG and sustainability reporting assurance

**The Everest story matters:** If asked about challenges facing the industry, you can show sophistication by referencing the attempted split — it demonstrates you follow professional services news.
