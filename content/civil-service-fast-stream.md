# Civil Service Fast Stream — ApprenticeEdge Prep Pack

## The Programme

**What it is:** The Civil Service Fast Stream is the UK government's flagship graduate development programme. At school leaver level, the equivalent entry point is the **Civil Service Higher Apprenticeship** — a direct route into the Senior Civil Service pipeline without a degree.

**Length:** 2 years (Higher Apprenticeship, Level 4)

**Salary:** £27,000–£30,000 starting (London); £24,000–£26,000 (national)

**Location:** London (most departments), plus regional postings across the UK

**Streams available at school leaver/apprentice level:**
- Government Communications Service Apprenticeship
- Digital, Data and Technology (DDaT) Fast Stream Apprenticeship
- HR Apprenticeship
- Finance Apprenticeship
- Project Delivery Apprenticeship

**Note:** The traditional "Fast Stream" itself is graduate-entry. School leavers access the Civil Service through Higher Apprenticeship routes, which feed into the same career ladder. After completing the apprenticeship, high performers can apply directly to the Fast Stream.

**Application window:** Opens October/November annually. The process is long — expect 4–6 months from application to offer.

## The Application Stages

### Stage 1 — Online Application
Standard form. The Civil Service uses the **Civil Service Success Profiles** framework for all applications. Every question maps to one of five elements:

- **Behaviours** — how you act in work situations
- **Strengths** — what you naturally do well and enjoy
- **Ability** — cognitive aptitude
- **Experience** — your background and track record
- **Technical** — job-specific skills (varies by role)

**At apprentice level, Behaviours and Strengths are weighted most heavily.**

### Stage 2 — Online Tests (Civil Service Judgement Test)
The Civil Service uses its own bespoke assessments:
- **Civil Service Judgement Test** — situational judgement in a government work context
- **Civil Service Verbal Test** — reading comprehension and language precision
- **Numerical Reasoning** (some streams)

**Tip:** The Judgement Test models Civil Service values — impartiality, integrity, objectivity, honesty, and democratic accountability. Answers that reflect these values score highest.

### Stage 3 — Video Interview or Telephone Interview
Structured around Civil Service Behaviours. You will be told in advance which behaviours are being assessed.

**The core Civil Service Behaviours (know all of these):**
1. Making Effective Decisions
2. Leadership
3. Communicating and Influencing
4. Working Together
5. Delivering at Pace
6. Developing Self and Others
7. Managing a Quality Service
8. Changing and Improving
9. Seeing the Big Picture
10. Handling a Changing Environment

**STAR format applies, but the Civil Service uses STAR-slightly-adapted:**
- Situation
- Task
- Action (the most important — go into detail)
- Result + what you'd do differently

**Tip:** Civil Service interviewers often ask "what would you do differently next time?" after your result. Always prepare this.

### Stage 4 — Assessment Centre
The Civil Service Assessment Centre varies by stream. Typically:

1. **Behaviour-based interview** — 45 mins, 3–4 behaviours assessed
2. **Group exercise** — policy scenario or public service case study
3. **Written exercise** — ministerial briefing note or policy analysis
4. **Presentation** — in some streams

**Civil Service-specific tip:** The written exercise often involves writing a "submission" or briefing note in Civil Service style — structured, concise, no jargon, recommendation at the top. Practise this format: Purpose → Key Points → Recommendation → Risks.

## Civil Service Core Competencies

Focus your preparation on these five behaviours — they appear in almost every Civil Service interview:

| Behaviour | What assessors look for |
|---|---|
| Making Effective Decisions | You gather evidence, consider options, make clear decisions with imperfect information |
| Communicating and Influencing | You tailor your message to the audience, listen actively, persuade without pressure |
| Working Together | You build relationships, collaborate across boundaries, value diverse perspectives |
| Delivering at Pace | You manage competing priorities, keep track of progress, deliver on time |
| Changing and Improving | You spot what isn't working and suggest or implement improvements |

## Commercial Awareness — The Civil Service Context

### History in Brief

The modern British Civil Service was born from the Northcote-Trevelyan Report of 1854, which recommended replacing the patronage system — where jobs went to friends of ministers — with open competitive examinations. The Civil Service Commission was established in 1855 to administer entry exams, creating a professional, politically neutral bureaucracy for the first time. This was a radical reform: for the first time, government jobs would go to the most capable candidates, not the best-connected ones.

Today the Civil Service employs around 500,000 people across 24 ministerial departments and dozens of agencies. It serves whichever party is in government, providing continuity of administration across elections. The Cabinet Secretary is the most senior civil servant and acts as the head of the Civil Service — currently Sir Chris Wormald. The Fast Stream is the Civil Service's flagship graduate and school leaver development programme, fast-tracking high-potential candidates into senior roles.

The Civil Service isn't a commercial business, but "awareness" still matters — they call it **"Seeing the Big Picture."**

**What you need to understand:**
- **How government works:** Parliament passes legislation. Ministers set policy. Civil servants advise ministers and implement policy. The Civil Service is politically neutral — it serves whichever government is in power.
- **The departments:** HMRC, Home Office, DWP, DHSC, MOD, DCMS, FCDO, HM Treasury, Cabinet Office — know what each does at a high level.
- **Current government priorities (2025–26):** Economic growth, NHS reform, planning reform, AI regulation, net zero, defence spending. Read GOV.UK news or the King's Speech for context.
- **Civil Service reform:** The ongoing drive to digitalise public services, reduce headcount, improve delivery. The Digital, Data and Technology stream is expanding.

**The Civil Service values — memorise these:**
- Integrity
- Honesty
- Objectivity
- Impartiality

Every answer you give should be consistent with these values. Civil servants are not allowed to be partisan.

## Why the Civil Service Over Private Sector

This question often comes up — interviewers want to know you've made a deliberate choice.

**Example:** *"I want my work to have impact at scale — decisions made in government affect millions of people, and that scale of consequence isn't available anywhere else at this stage of a career. I'm specifically drawn to [DDaT/Project Delivery/Finance] because [specific reason]. The Civil Service's commitment to developing people — the apprenticeship route to Fast Stream is a structured, transparent path — is also very different from the corporate world, where progression can be less clear."*

- [ ] Know all 10 Civil Service Behaviours — print them and annotate with your examples
- [ ] Prepare one STAR story per behaviour (10 stories minimum)
- [ ] Add "what would I do differently" to every story
- [ ] Understand Civil Service Success Profiles (gov.uk/guidance/success-profiles — free)
- [ ] Read the GOV.UK guidance for your specific stream
- [ ] Practice writing in Civil Service briefing note format
- [ ] Know the four Civil Service values: integrity, honesty, objectivity, impartiality
- [ ] Know which government department your chosen stream sits within
